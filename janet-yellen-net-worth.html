Janet Yellen Wiki Biography

Janet Yellen was born on the 13th August 1946 in Brooklyn, New York USA and is of Polish descent. Janet is best known in the world of economics, currently serving as a Chair of the Board of Governors of the Federal Reserve System since 3rd February 2014. She has been active since 1971.

Janet was raised in a Jewish family; her father was a physician, originally from the town of Suwalki, Poland. Regarding her education, Janet attended the Fort Hamilton High School, situated in Bay Ridge, a southwest part of Brooklyn, following which in 1963 Yellen became a student at Brown University, from which she graduated summa cum laude in 1967 with a degree in Economics. After that she enrolled at Yale University, and earned a PhD degree in 1971; her thesis was entitled “Employment, output and capital accumulation in an open economy: a disequilibrium approach”.

Janet`s first job was as an assistant professor at the prestigious Harvard University, working there from 1971 until 1976. While serving in that position, Janet reviewed the country`s economics to the last detail, which resulted in her ability to forecast the 2008 economic crisis.

Thanks to her remarkable skills, in 2010, Yellen was promoted to the Vice Chair of the Federal Reserve, succeeding Donald Kohn, as she won the voting 17 to 6. She spent four years as a Vice Chair, and in 2014 she was approved as the Chair of the Board of Governors of the Federal Reserve System, when President Barack Obama nominated her to succeed Ben Bernanke in that position. Thanks to that outcome, Janet became the first women in that position, and the first member of the Democratic Party since Paul Volcker, who was appointed as Chairman of the Federal Reserve in 1979.
