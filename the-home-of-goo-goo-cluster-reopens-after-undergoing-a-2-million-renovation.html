The Home Of Goo Goo Cluster Reopens After Undergoing A $2 Million Renovation

June 24, 2024
blog

The Candy Bar, Goo Goo Cluster, reopened last Friday, November 5, 2021, after a $2 million renovation to the store. The candy bar located in Nashville, TN, has been part of the town since 1912. People behind the store have promised caramel and chocolate-filled renovations, which cost the company $2 million. Beth Sachan of the Goo Goo Cluster store said that the store had been part of the history, and her entire family, including her Grandma, has her generations marked, and the store represents the South.

Goo Goo Cluster has been up and running for 100 years now, and it is the home of America's first combination candy bar. The store has been a staple of the Lower Broadway, which brings in locals and tourists together for their sweetness. All the locals are fond of the store and love the store as the store has been passed down from different generations already.

The transformation of the shop has given a Willy Wonka vibe to it. The Goo Goo Candy store had had this idea long before Milky Way and Snickers got the idea. The newly renovated store at 116 3rd Avenue S has lovely designs that children will love. The customer can customize their candies with a wide range of various items like potato chips and even Fruit Loops cereal. The store also features a full service Chocolate Bar menu that was inspired by Goo Goo's classic clusters, bonbons, seasonal shakes, and other confectionary treats from the store's specialty. These offerings are a new service from the store.

Renovation of the place also includes a 500 sq foot of premier classroom where the children can experience making their candies. People interested in this class to make your candies can sign up at the Goo Goo Candy's website. The interactive course will give a whole new experience to the customers. Goo Goo Candy store has also started offering boozy milkshakes for adults who can even have whiskey and wine pairings. The $2 million renovations were accounted for the design of your own dessert station, a full service chocolate bar for the customers, and a retail shop. The shop has been reopened to the public on November 5, 2021, and will be open every day from 10 in the morning until 6 in the evening.
