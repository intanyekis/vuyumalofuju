Hit Albums And Singles With Destiny's Child

Beyoncé ruled the music charts for years as a part of the multi-platinum R&B girl group Destiny's Child in the late 1990s, alongside Kelly Rowland and Michelle Williams.

They released a slew of hit songs, including "Say My Name," "Independent Women," and "Bootylicious." Destiny's Child, led by Knowles, sold more than 60 million records worldwide.

Destiny's Child introduced Knowles to fame and money at a young age, setting the financial groundwork for her future solo success. However, her journey to prosperity had only just begun.

Beyoncé's Solo Music Career

Business Ventures

Aside from successful music sales, Beyoncé has amassed a fortune through shrewd commercial initiatives such as her high-fashion House of Deréon label.

House of Deréon enhanced her income by combining sophistication and feminine power in revamped couture and accessible sportswear.

Parkwood Entertainment provides Beyoncé with creative autonomy and equity in her work by directing films, creating albums such as 2016's Lemonade, and securing an estimated $60 million Netflix agreement.

Starring In Major Hollywood Films

Visual Album: Lemonade

Beyoncé's innovative 2016 visual album Lemonade increased her net worth, leveling her up both creatively and commercially.

She peeled back mental layers in a 65-minute video that accompanied the twelve-track, thematically adventurous and musically complex Lemonade. It debuted at number one, selling close to five million copies by 2019.

Along with sumptuous production, evocative lyricism, and the representation of black perseverance through art, Lemonade criticized infidelity rumors, which amplified Beyoncé's global microphone.

The audacious smash combined musical brilliance with personal discovery, boosting record and ticket revenue. By 2019, the music and film had grossed $82 million combined.

Her honesty through song yielded commercial success, with Lemonade fulfilling artistic tastes, while fueling her empire's financial expansion.

Co-Headlining A Blockbuster Tour

During her thrilling 2018 On The Run II tour alongside husband Jay-Z (Shawn Corey Carter), Beyoncé solidified her reputation as a touring powerhouse.

In just 48 performances across North America and Europe, the power couple earned over $250 million. Through her enormous set lists, Beyoncé transformed lyrics into legendary production value, making it the second highest-grossing tour ever.

With the addition of colorful dancing and eye-catching fireworks, Beyoncé created an immersive experience that resulted in record-breaking sales.

The Carters proved to be unsurpassed in touring supremacy and financial returns while providing unrivaled live entertainment and expanding their combined empire.

Leveraging Her Brand In Production Deals

Recognizing her uncommon impact, Beyoncé has leveraged her talent for extraordinary production deals and to secure liberty of expression.

In 2015, she inked a $20 million contract with Pepsi, her most significant corporate collaboration. In 2016, the Ivy Park apparel brand debuted.

In 2020, Disney+ reportedly paid her $100 million for three separate projects. Following the video streaming achievements of Homecoming and Black Is King, Netflix agreed to a three-project, $60 million contract.

Beyoncé retains control over her work and owns her destiny as an artist and entrepreneur by owning these enterprises through her business, Parkwood Entertainment.
